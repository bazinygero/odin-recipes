Varenyky (pierogy, dumplings, etc.)

Varenyky are filled dumplings made by wrapping unleavened dough around a savoury or sweet filling and cooking in boiling water. They are often pan-fried before serving.

Varenyky are associated with the cuisines of Central, Eastern and Southeastern Europe, though they most likely originated in China and came to Europe in the Middle Ages. Varenyky are also popular in modern-day American and Canadian cuisine, where they are sometimes known under different local names.

Typical fillings include potato, cheese, quark, sauerkraut, ground meat, mushrooms, and/or fruits. Savoury pierogi are often served with a topping of sour cream, fried onions, or both.

Ingridients:

  • Dough (flour, warm water sometimes egg)
  • Mashed potatoes
  • Fried onions or fried bacon strips

Steps:

  1. Prepare a dough with mixing of warm watter combining it with flour and egg (if you like).
  2. Prepare the mashed potatoes for stuffing and wrap the small pieses of flat dough around the filling. Seal the dough with your fingers.
  3. They can be served with melted butter or sour cream, or garnished with small pieces of fried bacon, onions, and mushrooms.
